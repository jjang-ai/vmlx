2.  **Define "Temperature" in LLMs:**
    *   What is it? It's a hyperparameter controlling the randomness of the output.
    *   How does it work mathematically? It scales the logits (raw scores) before applying the softmax function.
    *   Low Temperature (near 0): Sharp probability distribution. High probability for the top token, low for others. Deterministic.
    *   High Temperature (near 1 or higher): Flatter probability distribution. More chance for lower-probability tokens. Stochastic/Creative.

3.  **Analyze Temperature = 0:**
    *   *Behavior:* Greedy decoding (effectively). Always picks the highest probability token.
    *   *Pros:* Deterministic, reproducible, consistent, good for logic/math/code where correctness is key.
    *   *Cons:* Repetitive, boring, can get stuck in loops, lacks nuance/creativity.
    *   *Use Cases:* Coding, data extraction, factual QA, math problems.

4.  **Analyze Temperature = 0.3:**
    *   *Behavior:* Slightly stochastic. Still favors high-probability tokens but allows some variance.
    *   *Pros:* More natural language flow, less robotic, reduces repetition, allows for slight variations in phrasing without losing coherence.
    *   *Cons:* Less reproducible, slight risk of hallucination or deviation from strict facts, might introduce minor errors in logic.
    *   *Use Cases:* Chatbots, creative writing, summarization, general assistance.
