2.  **Define "Temperature" in the context of LLMs:**
    *   What is it? A hyperparameter controlling randomness in token selection.
    *   How does it work mathematically? It scales the logits before the softmax function.
        *   $P(token) = \frac{e^{logit/token\_temp}}{\sum e^{logit/token\_temp}}$
    *   Low temp = sharper distribution (more deterministic).
    *   High temp = flatter distribution (more stochastic).

3.  **Analyze Temperature 0 (Deterministic):**
    *   *Behavior:* Always picks the token with the highest probability (argmax).
    *   *Pros:* Reproducibility, consistency, precision, factuality (usually), good for code/math/logic.
    *   *Cons:* Repetitive, rigid, might get stuck in loops, lacks creativity, "boring."
    *   *Use Cases:* Coding assistants, data extraction, strict formatting, factual Q&A where hallucination is critical.

4.  **Analyze Temperature 0.3 (Low-Moderate Stochasticity):**

    *   *Cons:* Slightly less consistent, potential for minor hallucinations or deviation from strict constraints, non-deterministic (harder to debug exact outputs).
    *   *Use Cases:* Chatbots, creative writing (light), summarization, general assistance.
