Understanding Temperature

Temperature is a sampling parameter that controls the randomness of token selection during text generation. It modifies the probability distribution over the next token:

$$P(token) = \frac{\exp(\logits / T)}{\sum \exp(\logits / T)}$$

Where T is the temperature parameter. Temperature = 0 (Deterministic/Greedy)

Behavior:
- Always selects the highest-probability token
- Completely deterministic: same input → same output every time
- No exploration of alternative token choices

Advantages:
- ✅ Maximum consistency and reproducibility
- ✅ Best for factual/analytical tasks
- ✅ Predictable outputs for testing and debugging
- ✅ Optimal for code generation where correctness matters

Disadvantages:
- ❌ Can produce repetitive, robotic text
- ❌ May get stuck in local optima
- ❌ Lacks creativity and variety
- ❌ Can feel mechanical in conversational contexts

Behavior:

Disadvantages:
