2.  **Define "Temperature" in the context of LLMs:**
    *   What is it? A hyperparameter controlling randomness in token selection.
    *   How does it work? It scales the logits (probabilities) before the softmax function.
    *   High Temp = Flatter distribution = More randomness/creativity.
    *   Low Temp = Sharper distribution = More deterministic/greedy.

3.  **Analyze Temperature 0:**
    *   *Mechanism:* Greedy decoding. Always picks the token with the highest probability.
    *   *Characteristics:* Deterministic, reproducible, focused, concise.
    *   *Pros:* Consistency, factual accuracy (usually), good for code/math, easy to cache.
    *   *Cons:* Repetitive loops, lack of nuance, "mode collapse" (stuck in one pattern), less creative.

4.  **Analyze Temperature 0.3:**
    *   *Mechanism:* Softmax with slight scaling. Allows lower-probability tokens a small chance.
    *   *Characteristics:* Slightly stochastic, varied, more natural flow.
    *   *Pros:* Better readability, less robotic, handles ambiguity better, avoids repetition loops.
    *   *Cons:* Less deterministic (same prompt $\neq$ same output), slightly higher risk of hallucination or deviation from instructions.

5.  **Identify the Tradeoff:**
    *   *Determinism vs. Variety:* 0 is fixed, 0.3 varies.

    *   *Risk:* 0 risks loops, 0.3 risks hallucinations.
